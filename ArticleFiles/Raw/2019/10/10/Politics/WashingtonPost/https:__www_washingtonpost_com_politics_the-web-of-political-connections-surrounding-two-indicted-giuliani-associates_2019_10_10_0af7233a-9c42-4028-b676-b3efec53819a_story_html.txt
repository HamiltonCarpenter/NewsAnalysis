1:34 PM EDT</div></div><div class="article-body"><div class="teaser-content"><section><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">The last time Lev Parnas and Igor Fruman were in the news, they were the targets of subpoenas from House Democrats as part of the impeachment inquiry involving President Trump. Parnas and Fruman are associates of Trump’s personal attorney Rudolph Giuliani. According to their attorney — Trump’s former attorney John Dowd — the pair are also represented by Giuliani, which conveniently shields their communications with Giuliani under attorney-client privilege.</p></div></section></div><div class="remainder-content"><section><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">If that set of interactions seems complex, buckle up.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">On Wednesday evening, Parnas and Fruman were <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/two-business-associates-of-trumps-personal-lawyer-giuliani-have-been-arrested-and-are-in-custody/2019/10/10/9f9c101a-eb63-11e9-9306-47cb0324fd44_story.html?tid=lk_inline_manual_4" target="_blank">arrested</a> in Virginia. The pair were two of four individuals named in an indictment obtained by federal prosecutors investigating campaign finance violations involving excessive campaign contributions and illegal foreign spending. What’s described <a href="https://www.documentcloud.org/documents/6466331-U-S-v-Lev-Parnas-Et-Al-Indictment.html" target="_blank">in the indictment</a> is a complex set of interactions and campaign payments with Parnas and Fruman at the center.</p></div><div><div></div></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">In walking through their interactions, it’s important to remember one bit of context. The impeachment inquiry targeting <b>Trump</b> focuses on his interactions with Ukrainian President <b>Volodymyr Zelensky</b>. During a call in late July, Trump asked that Zelensky and Ukraine investigate former vice president <b>Joe Biden</b>. That request was the culmination of an effort by <b>Giuliani</b> to elevate now-debunked allegations targeting the potential 2020 Democratic nominee. Giuliani’s investigation was itself aided by <b>Parnas </b>and<b> Fruman</b>. During the Trump-Zelensky call, the pair also discussed former U.S. ambassador to Ukraine <b>Marie Yovanovitch</b>.</p></div><div><div class="cb bg-offwhite mt-xxs pad-top-md pad-bottom-md mb-lg ml-neg-gutter mr-neg-gutter mr-auto-ns ml-auto-ns dn db-ns relative" style="min-height:250px"><div class="absolute z-0" style="left:50%;top:50%">AD</div><div class="relative z-1"><div class=""><wp-ad class="hide-for-print hide-for-print" id="slug_inline_bb"></wp-ad></div></div></div></div><div><div class="cb bg-offwhite mt-xxs pad-top-md pad-bottom-md mb-lg ml-neg-gutter mr-neg-gutter mr-auto-ns ml-auto-ns db dn-ns relative" style="min-height:250px"><div class="absolute z-0" style="left:50%;top:50%">AD</div><div class="relative z-1"><div class=""><wp-ad class="hide-for-print hide-for-print" id="slug_mob_bigbox"></wp-ad></div></div></div></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">The indictment focuses on a few specific incidents involving Parnas and Fruman.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">One part of the indictment involves their interactions with an unnamed politician, identified in later reporting as former congressman <b>Pete Sessions</b> of Texas. Sessions (R) was the beneficiary of political contributions from Parnas, including a contribution that the indictment alleges was made with money provided by Fruman. Fruman and Parnas pushed Sessions to call for Yovanovitch’s ouster as ambassador. Sessions <a href="https://www.scribd.com/document/402558867/Sessions-Pompeo-correspondence-2018" target="_blank">sent a letter</a> to Secretary of State Mike Pompeo in May 2018 calling for Yovanovitch to be fired. The same day the letter was sent, Parnas <a href="https://www.huffpost.com/entry/lev-parnas-igor-fruman-giuliani-trump_n_5d9f32f1e4b02c9da045a4c2?mso" target="_blank">posted</a> a now-deleted photo of himself with Sessions. The indictment suggests that the effort to oust Yovanovitch by the pair came in part from pressure by a Ukrainian politician.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">Sessions also <a href="https://www.thedailybeast.com/america-first-action-backed-by-giuliani-henchmen-spent-big-for-pete-sessions-after-he-targeted-ukraine-diplo" target="_blank">received money</a> from a PAC called <b>America First Action</b>. That PAC was a beneficiary of $325,000 contributed by a company called <b>Global Energy Producers</b>, which the indictment alleges was created to shield the sources of the contributions: Parnas and Fruman.
