But despite Trump’s high level of support in the GOP, there has long been reason to believe <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/monkey-cage/wp/2018/08/07/trumps-base-is-weaker-than-it-seems-our-new-study-finds/?tid=lk_inline_manual_22" target="_blank">there is something of a soft underbelly there</a>, with many Republicans <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2019/08/20/trump-hailed-his-support-poorly-educated-they-might-be-turning-him/?tid=lk_inline_manual_22" target="_blank">liking but not loving Trump</a> and even <a href="https://www.axios.com/poll-trump-dislike-approval-rating-2020ppp-election-f3db749a-717c-4a91-9543-027d9deca386.html" target="_blank">disliking him personally</a>. It’s possible many Republicans like Trump and even approve of his job performance but think there are legitimate questions here.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">The party as a whole isn’t suddenly going to jump on board with impeaching and removing Trump.
