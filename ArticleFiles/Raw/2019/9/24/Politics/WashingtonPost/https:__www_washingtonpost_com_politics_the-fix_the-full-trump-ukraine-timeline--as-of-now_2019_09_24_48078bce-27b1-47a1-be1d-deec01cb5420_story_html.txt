learned late Monday that President Trump <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/trump-ordered-hold-on-military-aid-days-before-calling-ukrainian-president-officials-say/2019/09/23/df93a6ca-de38-11e9-8dc8-498eabc129a0_story.html" target="_blank">ordered the withholding of military aid to Ukraine</a> about one week before his phone call with Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky on July 25. It’s a detail that will add to questions about whether Trump was tying the aid to the Eastern European country investigating former vice president Joe Biden and his son Hunter.</p> <p data-elm-loc="2">A whistleblower complaint alleges that someone — apparently Trump — might have made an inappropriate “promise” to a foreign leader. Trump has denied this, but he has admitted to discussing the Bidens with Zelensky on that call, and he has suggested that <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2019/09/23/trump-inches-toward-spilling-beans-ukraine/" target="_blank">it would be okay for him to withhold money</a> from a country that doesn’t root out “corruption.”</p> <p data-elm-loc="3">The situation has Democrats inching closer to impeachment of Trump, whom they accuse of leveraging his office for personal political gain.</p> <p data-elm-loc="4">With everything evolving quickly, we’ve put together the following timeline. It will be updated as needed.</p> <h2 data-elm-loc="5">2014-2015</h2> <p data-elm-loc="6"><b>Feb. 22, 2014.</b> Ukrainian President Viktor Yanukovych is ousted from power during a popular uprising in the country. He flees to Russia. Yanukovych came to power with the assistance of political consultant Paul Manafort, who worked for Yanukovych’s Party of Regions.</p> <p data-elm-loc="7">After his ouster, Ukrainian officials begin a <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-05-16/ukraine-prosecutor-says-no-evidence-of-wrongdoing-by-bidens" target="_blank">wide-ranging investigation</a> into corruption in the country.</p> <p data-elm-loc="8"><b>May 13, 2014.</b> Hunter Biden, the son of then-Vice President Joe Biden, <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/worldviews/wp/2014/05/14/hunter-bidens-new-job-at-a-ukrainian-gas-company-is-a-problem-for-u-s-soft-power/" target="_blank">joins the board</a> of the Ukrainian energy company Burisma Holdings. It is owned by oligarch Mykola Zlochevsky, one of several subjects of the Ukrainian corruption probe.</p> <p data-elm-loc="9"><b>Sept. 24, 2015.</b> The U.S. ambassador to Ukraine, Geoffrey Pyatt, <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20150926092251/http://ukraine.usembassy.gov/statements/ambpyatt-odesa-finforum-09242015.html" target="_blank">blasts</a> Ukraine Prosecutor General Viktor Shokin in a speech in Odessa, Ukraine. He points to a “glaring problem” that threatens the good work regional leaders are doing: “the failure of the institution of the Prosecutor General of Ukraine to successfully fight internal corruption."</p> <p data-elm-loc="10">“The United States stands behind those who challenge these bad actors,” Pyatt adds.</p> <p data-elm-loc="11"><b>Dec. 9, 2015.</b> <a href="https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/the-press-office/2015/12/09/remarks-vice-president-joe-biden-ukrainian-rada" target="_blank">In Kiev</a>, Biden tells Ukrainian leaders to fire Shokin or lose more than $1 billion in loan guarantees. Biden joins many Western leaders in urging Shokin’s ouster.</p> <h2 data-elm-loc="12">2016</h2> <p data-elm-loc="13"><b>Feb. 10, 2016. </b>The International Monetary Fund <a href="https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/feb/10/imf-warns-ukraine-halt-40bn-bailout-corruption-christine-lagarde" target="_blank">threatens</a> to halt a bailout program for Ukraine unless the country addresses its corruption issues.</p> <p data-elm-loc="14"><b>March 29, 2016.</b> Shokin is <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2016/03/30/world/europe/political-stability-in-the-balance-as-ukraine-ousts-top-prosecutor.html" target="_blank">ousted</a> from his position by Ukraine’s parliament.</p> <p data-elm-loc="15">The same day, Manafort is hired by Donald Trump’s presidential campaign to help in securing Republican National Convention delegates.</p> <p data-elm-loc="16"><b>May 12, 2016.</b> Yuriy Lutsenko becomes prosecutor general of Ukraine, replacing Shokin.</p> <p data-elm-loc="17"><b>June 20, 2016</b>. Manafort becomes the head of Trump’s campaign after campaign manager Corey Lewandowski is fired.</p> <p data-elm-loc="18"><b>Aug. 14, 2016.</b> Ukrainian officials <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2016/08/15/us/politics/paul-manafort-ukraine-donald-trump.html" target="_blank">reveal</a> the existence of a handwritten ledger suggesting that Manafort had received off-the-books payments from Yanukovych’s political party to the tune of millions of dollars. These payments are ultimately part of criminal charges filed against Manafort in the United States.</p> <p data-elm-loc="19"><b>Aug. 19, 2016.</b> Manafort is forced out from Trump’s campaign.</p> <p data-elm-loc="20"><b>Nov. 8, 2016.</b> Donald Trump is elected president.</p> <h2 data-elm-loc="21">2017—2018</h2> <p data-elm-loc="22"><b>Jan. 12, 2017.</b> Probes of Burisma are finalized and closed, <a href="https://www.kyivpost.com/business-wire/cases-closed-burisma-group-president-nikolay-zlochevskyi-ukraine-company-cooperated-law-enforcement-agencies-paid-full-outstanding-fees.html" target="_blank">according to the company</a>, though Lutsenko later tells <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-05-16/ukraine-prosecutor-says-no-evidence-of-wrongdoing-by-bidens" target="_blank">Bloomberg</a> that one sale of an oil storage terminal will still be investigated.</p> <p data-elm-loc="23"><b>Jan. 23, 2018.</b> At an event hosted by the Council on Foreign Relations, Biden <a href="https://www.cfr.org/event/foreign-affairs-issue-launch-former-vice-president-joe-biden" target="_blank">describes</a> the pressure he put on Ukraine’s government.</p> <p data-elm-loc="24">“They were walking out to a press conference. I said, ‘Nah, I’m not going to — or, we’re not going to give you the billion dollars.’ They said: ‘You have no authority. You’re not the president. I said, 'Call him,’ ” Biden says. “I said, ‘I’m telling you, you’re not getting the billion dollars.’ I said: ‘You’re not getting the billion. I’m going to be leaving here in, I think it was about six hours.’ I looked at them and said: ‘I’m leaving in six hours. If the prosecutor is not fired, you’re not getting the money.’ Well, son of a b----, he got fired. And they put in place someone who was solid at the time.” </p> <p data-elm-loc="25"><b>May 2, 2018. </b>The New York Times <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2018/05/02/world/europe/ukraine-mueller-manafort-missiles.html" target="_blank">reports</a> that Ukrainian officials had decided to halt assisting special counsel Robert S. Mueller III’s investigation out of concern that doing so would harm their relationship with Trump’s administration.</p> <p data-elm-loc="26"><b>May 4, 2018.</b> Three Democratic senators -- Bob Menendez (N.J.), Richard Durbin (Ill.) and Patrick Leahy (Vt.) -- <a href="https://www.foreign.senate.gov/imo/media/doc/5-4-18%20Menendez%20joint%20letter%20to%20General%20Prosecutor%20of%20Ukraine%20on%20Mueller%20investigation.pdf" target="_blank">write to Lutsenko</a> urging him to explain and to continue working with Mueller.</p> <h2 data-elm-loc="27">2019</h2> <p data-elm-loc="28"><b>March 20.</b> Lutsenko <a href="https://thehill.com/hilltv/rising/434875-top-ukrainian-justice-official-says-us-ambassador-gave-him-a-do-not-prosecute" target="_blank">alleges</a> U.S. Ambassador to Ukraine Marie Yovanovitch gave him “a list of people whom we should not prosecute” in an interview with the Hill. The State Department calls the claim an “outright fabrication,” but Trump promotes the story in a tweet.</p> <div class="col-print-12" data-elm-loc="29"> <div class="element element-oembed"> <blockquote class="twitter-tweet"><p dir="ltr" lang="en">“John Solomon: As Russia Collusion fades, Ukrainian plot to help Clinton emerges.” <a href="https://twitter.com/seanhannity?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">@seanhannity</a> <a href="https://twitter.com/FoxNews?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">@FoxNews</a></p>— Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) <a href="https://twitter.com/realDonaldTrump/status/1108559080204001280?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">March 21, 2019</a></blockquote> </div> </div> <p data-elm-loc="30"><b>April. </b>Hunter Biden’s term as a board member ends, and he leaves Burisma.</p> <p data-elm-loc="31"><b>April 18.</b> Mueller <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/2019/politics/read-the-mueller-report/" target="_blank">releases</a> his report detailing his team’s investigation into Russian interference in the 2016 election.</p> <p data-elm-loc="32">Lutsenko <a href="https://www.unian.info/politics/10520715-ukraine-prosecutor-general-lutsenko-admits-u-s-ambassador-didn-t-give-him-a-do-not-prosecute-list.html" target="_blank">retracts</a> his claim that Yovanovitch gave him a list of people not to prosecute.</p> <p data-elm-loc="33"><b>April 21.</b> Zelensky, a former TV comedian, is elected president of Ukraine with 73 percent of the vote. Trump calls him to offer his congratulations.</p> <p data-elm-loc="34"><b>May 7.</b> Yovanovitch is <a href="https://foreignpolicy.com/2019/05/07/us-ambassador-to-ukraine-recalled-in-political-hit-job-lawmakers-say-marie-yovanovitch-lutsenko-right-wing-media-accusations-congress-diplomats-diplomacy/" target="_blank">recalled</a> by the State Department, two months before her scheduled departure date. Democrats allege a “political hit job” aimed at creating a pretext to remove her.</p> <p data-elm-loc="35">In his later July 25 phone call with Zelensky, Trump will say, according to a tough transcript provided by the White House, “The former ambassador from the United States, the woman, was bad news, and the people she was dealing with in the Ukraine were bad news so I just want to let you know that." When Zelensky thanks Trump for previously warning him about Yovanovitch (exactly when that occurred isn’t clear), Trump responds, “Well, she’s going to go through some things.”</p> <p data-elm-loc="36"><b>Some time in May.</b> Giuliani meets with a top Ukrainian anti-corruption prosecutor, Nazar Kholodnytsky, in Paris, according to Kholodnytsky. Kholodnytsky, who had clashed with Yovanovitch, has declined to comment on what he and Guiliani discussed, but he said the Burisma investigation should be reopened.</p> <p data-elm-loc="37"><b>May 9.</b> Rudolph W. Giuliani <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2019/05/09/us/politics/giuliani-ukraine-trump.html" target="_blank">says</a> he will travel to Ukraine to push for investigations “because that information will be very, very helpful to my client, and may turn out to be helpful to my government.”</p> <p data-elm-loc="38"><b>May 11.</b> Giuliani <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2019/05/10/giulianis-remarkable-admission-about-seeking-ukraines-assistance-trump/" target="_blank">cancels</a> his Ukraine trip amid a backlash over the propriety of a personal aide to the president seeking damaging information about a political opponent from a foreign government.</p> <p data-elm-loc="39"><b>May 16.</b> Lutsenko <a href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2019-05-16/ukraine-prosecutor-says-no-evidence-of-wrongdoing-by-bidens" target="_blank">says</a> there is no evidence of any wrongdoing by the Bidens.</p> <p data-elm-loc="40"><b>May 19.</b> In an interview with Fox News, Trump <a href="https://factba.se/transcript/donald-trump-interview-steve-hilton-fox-telephone-may-19-2019" target="_blank">explicitly</a> references Biden’s efforts in Ukraine.</p> <p data-elm-loc="41">“Biden, he calls them and says ‘Don’t you dare persecute, if you don’t fire this prosecutor’ — The prosecutor was after his son,” Trump says. “Then he said ‘If you fire the prosecutor, you’ll be okay. And if you don’t fire the prosecutor, 'we’re not giving you $2 billion in loan guarantees,’ or whatever he was supposed to give. Can you imagine if I did that?”</p> <p data-elm-loc="42">Again, the Burisma probe was dormant at the time, according to U.S. and Ukrainian officials, and there was <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2019/05/23/fact-checking-president-trumps-wild-jabs-joe-biden/?tid=lk_inline_manual_18" target="_blank">no evidence</a> it focused on any actions by the Bidens.</p> <p data-elm-loc="43"><b>May 20.</b> Zelensky is inaugurated as president of Ukraine.</p> <p data-elm-loc="44"><b>May 23.</b> The administration notifies Congress that it intends to release aid money to Ukraine.</p> <p data-elm-loc="45"><b>June 13.</b> In an <a href="https://abcnews.go.com/Politics/id-exclusive-interview-trump-listen-foreigners-offered-dirt/story?id=63669304&amp;cid=clicksource_4380645_null" target="_blank">interview</a> with ABC News’s George Stephanopoulos, Trump says he might accept electoral assistance from a foreign government, if offered.</p> <p data-elm-loc="46">“I think you might want to listen, there isn’t anything wrong with listening,” Trump says. “If somebody called from a country, Norway, ‘We have information on your opponent'? Oh, I think I’d want to hear it.”</p> <p data-elm-loc="47">The chairwoman of the Federal Election Commission subsequently <a href="https://twitter.com/EllenLWeintraub/status/1139309394968096768" target="_blank">points out</a> on Twitter that this would be illegal.</p> <p data-elm-loc="48"><b>July 12.</b> Axios <a href="https://www.axios.com/donald-trump-remove-dan-coats-director-of-national-intelligence-2ba4275d-7624-4f4b-9026-ee0d0606ce32.html" target="_blank">reports</a> that Trump and Director of National Intelligence Daniel Coats are at odds, with Trump telling confidants that he wants to remove Coats from his position.</p> <p data-elm-loc="49"><b>July 18.</b> Trump’s decision to withhold nearly $400 million in aid to Ukraine is <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/trump-ordered-hold-on-military-aid-days-before-calling-ukrainian-president-officials-say/2019/09/23/df93a6ca-de38-11e9-8dc8-498eabc129a0_story.html" target="_blank">communicated</a> to the State and Defense departments. Members of Congress are told that the hold is part of an “interagency delay.”</p> <p data-elm-loc="50"><b>July 22.</b> Shokin <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/as-vice-president-biden-said-ukraine-should-increase-gas-production-then-his-son-got-a-job-with-a-ukrainian-gas-company/2019/07/21/f599f42c-86dd-11e9-98c1-e945ae5db8fb_story.html" target="_blank">tells</a> The Post that he was removed over the Biden issue.</p> <p data-elm-loc="51">“I will answer that the activities of Burisma, the involvement of his son, Hunter Biden, and the [prosecutor general’s office] investigators on his tail, are the only, I emphasize, the only motives for organizing my resignation,” he says. Other Ukrainian officials have cast doubt on this and said the investigation had long been dormant when Shokin was removed.</p> <p data-elm-loc="52"><b>July 24.</b> Mueller <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/mueller-testimony-congress-live/2019/07/24/d51a82d6-aca1-11e9-bc5c-e73b603e7f38_story.html" target="_blank">testifies</a> before Congress about his report and its findings.</p> <p data-elm-loc="53"><b>July 25.</b> Trump and Zelensky speak on the phone. As we later find out from <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/2019/09/25/rough-transcript-trumps-call-with-ukraines-president-annotated/?sdseew" target="_blank">a rough transcript</a> released by the White House, Trump repeatedly notes how “good” the United States is to Ukraine and then proceeds to ask Zelensky to open two investigations -- one involving CrowdStrike, an internet security company that probed the Democratic National Committee hack in 2016, and the other involving the Bidens.</p> <p data-elm-loc="54">“I would like you to do us a favor though because our country has been through a lot and Ukraine knows a lot about it,” Trump says before floating the CrowdStrike investigation.</p> <p data-elm-loc="55">He later adds: “The other thing, there’s a lot of talk about Biden’s son, that Biden stopped the prosecution and a lot of people want to find out about that so whatever you can do with the Attorney General would be great. Biden went around bragging that he stopped the prosecution so if you can look into it ... It sounds horrible to me.”</p> <p data-elm-loc="56">Trump repeatedly suggests Attorney General William P. Barr will be involved in working with the Ukrainian government on the investigation.</p> <p data-elm-loc="57">Zelensky tells Trump that his yet-to-be-named new prosecutor general “will look into the situation, specifically to the company that you mentioned in this issue" -- referring to Burisma.</p> <p data-elm-loc="58"><b>July 28.</b> Trump <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/national-security/intelligence-director-coats-expected-to-resign/2019/07/28/34e361e2-b16b-11e9-8e94-71a35969e4d8_story.html" target="_blank">announces</a> that Coats will resign in August.</p> <p data-elm-loc="59"><b>July 31</b>. Trump holds a <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/video/politics/trump-says-he-did-not-discuss-election-interference-with-putin/2019/08/01/3ba87b13-9a80-4db3-967d-f5575933a1d2_video.html" target="_blank">phone call</a> with Russian President Vladimir Putin. The call is first reported by the Russians; the White House doesn’t confirm it until late that evening. The Russians, in a much more substantial readout than the United States, claim Trump and Putin spoke about restoring full relations one day.</p> <p data-elm-loc="60"><b>Early August.</b> At some point this month, Giuliani travels to Spain to meet with a representative of the Zelensky government. <a href="https://www.nytimes.com/2019/08/21/us/politics/giuliani-ukraine.html" target="_blank">According</a> to the New York Times, that meeting involved Giuliani’s encouraging Zelensky’s government to investigate Hunter Biden.</p> <p data-elm-loc="61"><b>Aug. 3.</b> Zelensky <a href="https://www.kyivpost.com/ukraine-politics/zelensky-plans-to-meet-with-trump-in-september.html" target="_blank">announces</a> that he will travel to the United States to meet with Trump in Washington in September.</p> <p data-elm-loc="62"><b>Aug. 8.</b> Trump <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/no-2-intelligence-official-resigning-trump-announces/2019/08/08/9ed9d266-b54e-11e9-951e-de024209545d_story.html" target="_blank">announces</a> Joseph Maguire will take Coats’s job as director of national intelligence in an acting capacity. In doing so, he bypasses Sue Gordon, who had been Coats’s No. 2 at the directorate of national intelligence and was a career intelligence official with bipartisan support. Gordon would later resign.</p> <p data-elm-loc="63"><b>Aug. 12.</b> An anonymous whistleblower files a complaint with the inspector general for the intelligence community. The contents of the whistleblower complaint are still secret. Intelligence Community Inspector General Michael Atkinson will later determine the complaint to be credible and a matter of “urgent concern,” triggering a legally required disclosure to the House and Senate intelligence committees.</p> <p data-elm-loc="64"><b>Aug. 15.</b> Coats and Gordon officially leave their positions.</p> <p data-elm-loc="65"><b>Late August.</b> Lawmakers raise concerns about Ukraine aid being withheld, <a href="https://www.politico.com/story/2019/08/28/trump-ukraine-military-aid-russia-1689531" target="_blank">citing</a> its importance to defend the former Soviet republic from Russia.</p> <p data-elm-loc="66"><b>Sept. 1.</b> Zelensky and Vice President Pence <a href="https://www.president.gov.ua/en/news/volodimir-zelenskij-obgovoriv-z-vice-prezidentom-ssha-vijsko-57065" target="_blank">meet</a> as various world leaders are in Poland for a ceremony commemorating World War II. Trump had originally been slated to attend the ceremony but remained in the United States to monitor Hurricane Dorian.</p> <p data-elm-loc="67"><b>Sept. 2.</b> Pence says he didn’t discuss Biden with Zelensky, but that he did <a href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefings-statements/remarks-vice-president-pence-president-duda-poland-joint-press-conference-warsaw-poland/" target="_blank">suggest that aid was contingent on rooting out corruption</a>.</p> <p data-elm-loc="68">“As President Trump had me make clear, we have great concerns about issues of corruption,” Pence said. “The president wants to be assured that those resources are truly making their way to the kind of investments that will contribute to security and stability in Ukraine.”</p> <p data-elm-loc="69"><b>Early September</b>. Sen. Chris Murphy (D-Conn.) travels to Ukraine and meets with Zelensky.
