Elizabeth Warren (D-Mass.) fielded questions about Medicare-for-all at a town hall last week, then faced inquiries from reporters on impeachment. Former vice president Joe Biden spent a few minutes discussing impeachment at a recent stop before turning to an abbreviated version of his usual speech.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">“It definitely throws a monkey wrench into everybody’s talking points,” said Rep. Matthew Cartwright (D-Pa.), who supports Biden and endorsed an impeachment inquiry for the first time last week. “It’s something extra that they<span> have to talk about.”</span></p></div><div><div></div></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">While leading Democrats increasingly demand forceful statements on Trump’s actions, many voters and activists are urging candidates not to stop talking about how they would provide health insurance to more Americans, shrink the gap between rich and poor, and combat climate change. Some bluntly warn that devoting too much attention to impeachment could be a costly mistake.</p></div><div><div class="cb bg-offwhite mt-xxs pad-top-md pad-bottom-md mb-lg ml-neg-gutter mr-neg-gutter mr-auto-ns ml-auto-ns dn db-ns relative" style="min-height:250px"><div class="absolute z-0" style="left:50%;top:50%">AD</div><div class="relative z-1"><div class=""><wp-ad class="hide-for-print hide-for-print" id="slug_inline_bb"></wp-ad></div></div></div></div><div><div class="cb bg-offwhite mt-xxs pad-top-md pad-bottom-md mb-lg ml-neg-gutter mr-neg-gutter mr-auto-ns ml-auto-ns db dn-ns relative" style="min-height:250px"><div class="absolute z-0" style="left:50%;top:50%">AD</div><div class="relative z-1"><div class=""><wp-ad class="hide-for-print hide-for-print" id="slug_mob_bigbox"></wp-ad></div></div></div></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">“Impeachment is an afterthought for a lot of people,” said Elesha Gayman, chair of the Scott County Democratic Party in Iowa. “I appreciate them coming out with a statement, but that’s about where it needs to end. I think the most important thing to carry forward for the Democrats is going to be having a message and a vision for what they are going to bring to the table.”</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">She added, “I think, especially in the Democratic Party, we lose elections when we make it about ‘we’re better than the other guy’ and we don’t lay out a vision.”</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">What’s clear is that the furor in Washington injects another huge dose of uncertainty, at a critical moment, into a contest that was already highly fluid. The field is likely to winnow soon, and the impeachment focus threatens to take even more oxygen from hopefuls who already had limited time to make an impact.</p></div><div><div class="cb bg-offwhite mt-xxs pad-top-md pad-bottom-md mb-lg ml-neg-gutter mr-neg-gutter mr-auto-ns ml-auto-ns dn db-ns relative" style="min-height:250px"><div class="absolute z-0" style="left:50%;top:50%">AD</div><div class="relative z-1"><div class=""><wp-ad class="hide-for-print hide-for-print" id="slug_inline_bb_2"></wp-ad></div></div></div></div><div><div class="cb bg-offwhite mt-xxs pad-top-md pad-bottom-md mb-lg ml-neg-gutter mr-neg-gutter mr-auto-ns ml-auto-ns db dn-ns relative" style="min-height:250px"><div class="absolute z-0" style="left:50%;top:50%">AD</div><div class="relative z-1"><div class=""><wp-ad class="hide-for-print hide-for-print" id="slug_mob_bigbox_2"></wp-ad></div></div></div></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">House Speaker Nancy Pelosi (D-Calif.) took the rare step of <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/powerpost/pelosi-top-democrats-privately-discuss-creation-of-select-committee-for-impeachment/2019/09/24/af6f735a-dedf-11e9-b199-f638bf2c340f_story.html?tid=lk_inline_manual_15">beginning impeachment proceedings</a> against Trump last week after he acknowledged urging the president of Ukraine to investigate Biden and his son Hunter Biden. Many Democrats say dislodging Trump from office is unlikely, given the strong support for him in the Republican-controlled Senate, where a two-thirds vote is needed for removal.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">Nevertheless, the Democratic presidential field has largely lined up behind Pelosi, endorsing her inquiry and vocally condemning Trump’s conduct. A <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/house-intelligence-committee-releases-whistleblowers-complaint-citing-trumps-call-with-ukraines-president/2019/09/26/402052ee-e056-11e9-be96-6adb81821e90_story.html?tid=lk_inline_manual_16">whistleblower complaint</a> at the center of the controversy alleges that Trump misused his office for personal gain and that unidentified White House officials took improper actions to keep it a secret. Trump has said he did nothing wrong.</p></div><div><div><div><div class="poda-player-container" data-img="" data-org="washpost" data-slug="how-2020-democrats-are-navigating-the-impeachment-inquiry" data-sub="True" data-title=""> <!--script defer src="https://d2lhouwh993x2g.cloudfront.net/prod/loader.js"></script--> </div></div></div></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">Some voters expressed concerns about the Democrats forging ahead on impeachment. divide the country.</p></div><div><div class="cb bg-offwhite mt-xxs pad-top-md pad-bottom-md mb-lg ml-neg-gutter mr-neg-gutter mr-auto-ns ml-auto-ns dn db-ns relative" style="min-height:250px"><div picket line in front of a GM plant in Detroit last week with Sanders, said he worried that focusing on impeachment could take attention away from other issues.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">“Our focus is on this,” said Norona, 43, speaking of the autoworkers’ strike.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">But other Democrats, believing Trump to be increasingly vulnerable, want to see the candidates call him out more aggressively than ever.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">“They’ve got to keep the focus on Trump while hot lights are on him,” said Gilda Cobb-Hunter, president of the National Black Caucus of State Legislators and a veteran South Carolina Democrat.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">Shawana Love, 42, an undecided voter who also joined the picket line in Detroit, said seeing the Democrats take steps toward impeaching Trump gets her more excited about the election. “Impeach his a--!” Love said.</p></div><div><div class="cb bg-offwhite mt-xxs pad-top-md pad-bottom-md mb-lg ml-neg-gutter mr-neg-gutter mr-auto-ns ml-auto-ns dn db-ns relative" style="min-height:250px"><div class="absolute z-0" style="left:50%;top:50%">AD</div><div class="relative z-1"><div class=""><wp-ad class="hide-for-print hide-for-print" id="slug_inline_bb_4"></wp-ad></div></div></div></div><div><div class="cb font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">The fast-moving developments and clashing voter sentiments are prompting many of the candidates to dedicate valuable time at campaign events — and devote space on their social media pages — to responding to the latest Trump news with outrage.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">But they are taking different tacks.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">Warren has been pointing out that she advocated impeachment proceedings long before the recent revelations about Trump’s conversation with the Ukrainian president. Warren has been rising in the polls, and some of her rivals have begun criticizing her policies, but the impeachment furor could make that harder with so much attention focused on Trump.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">“The way to hold this president of the United States accountable is to impeach him,” Warren told reporters after Friday’s town hall. “So I hope we go forward with care and deliberation, but that we do it quickly. At the same time, Gifford said, it could prompt some Democrats to question whether the Ukraine situation would be a vulnerability for Biden — chipping away at his assertion that he’s the most electable Democrat — and “those questions have to be answered.”</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined">In the meantime, the drama set to unfold in coming weeks — likely to feature explosive congressional hearings, angry partisan debates, bitterly contested votes on the House floor and possibly further revelations about Trump — adds a daunting, unpredictable dynamic that, even more than the campaign so far, is outside the candidates’ control.</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md undefined"><span><span>As South Bend, Ind., Mayor Pete Buttigieg said r</span>ecently, “Nobody knows what the political consequences of impeachment </span>are.”</p></div><div><p class="font--body font-copy color-gray-darkest ma-0 pad-bottom-md italic">Annie Linskey in Hollis, N.H., Chelsea Janes in Plymouth, N.H., Cleve R.
